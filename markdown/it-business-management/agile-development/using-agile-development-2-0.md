# Using Agile Development 2.0

Learn how to use the features of Agile Development 2.0 to efficiently manage your product development life cycle.

-   **[Create a release in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/create-a-release.md)**  
Create a release to start associating the stories and scrum tasks of your product development to the release in Agile Development 2.0.
-   **[Create a theme in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/create-a-theme.md)**  
Create a theme in Agile Development 2.0 to group stories of different products together for an abstract goal.
-   **[Create a product in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/create-a-product.md)**  
Create a product to represent a feature or functionality important to customers. A product can contain themes, epics, and stories that describe these enhancements from the perspective of a user.
-   **[Assignment groups in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/manage-groups-agile-dev.md)**  
Learn the requirements to create and manage your assignment groups in Agile Development 2.0.
-   **[Create an epic in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/create-an-epic.md)**  
Define the high-level requirements of the features that you want to develop by creating epics in Agile Development 2.0.
-   **[Create a story in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/create-a-story.md)**  
Create high-level definitions of your requirements in the form of stories in Agile Development 2.0. Manage and track your stories by associating them to a product, project, or release and by assigning them to an assignment group.
-   **[Generate acceptance criteria for stories using Now Assist for Strategic Portfolio Management \(SPM\)](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/generate-acceptance-criteria-for-stories.md)**  
Create clear, comprehensive, and testable acceptance criteria for user stories without writing them manually in Agile Development 2.0. Review and refine suggested options to ensure they meet your requirements.
-   **[Add dependencies for Agile Development 2.0 stories](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/Add-dependencies-to-stories.md)**  
Add prerequisite and dependent items for stories in Agile Development 2.0 so that you can visualize these dependencies on the scrum planning board and plan your sprints accordingly.
-   **[Create a scrum task for Agile Development 2.0 stories](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/create-a-scrum-task.md)**  
Create scrum tasks in Agile Development 2.0 to break down a story into manageable pieces for your team to work on. You can create one scrum task at a time or multiple scrum tasks at once.
-   **[Create a personalized backlog in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/create-a-backlog.md)**  
Create filters to define which stories appear in your backlog in Agile Development 2.0.
-   **[Manage your product backlog in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/manage-maintain-backlog.md)**  
Manage your product backlog from a centralized location called the Agile board in Agile Development 2.0. Add stories to a personalized backlog and prioritize them as needed.
-   **[Plan your sprint activities in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/plan-sprint-activities.md)**  
Streamline your sprint activities such as creating, editing, and completing sprints, or adding stories to sprints, using the Sprint Planning tab of the Agile board.
-   **[Create sprints in bulk in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/create-agile-development-2-sprint-from-assignment-group.md)**  
Create multiple sprints at once for one or more Agile Development 2.0 assignment groups to save time.
-   **[Track progress of stories for a sprint in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/agile-sprint-story-board.md)**  
Use the Sprint Tracking tab of the Agile board in Agile Development 2.0 to track the development of the stories from your current sprint.
-   **[Track progress of scrum tasks for a sprint in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/agile-sprint-board.md)**  
Use the Sprint Tracking tab of the Agile board in Agile Development 2.0 to track the progress of scrum tasks for the stories from your current sprint.
-   **[Track sprint progress in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/sprint-tracking-list.md)**  
Track the progress of stories, scrum tasks, and tests for the current sprint from a single consolidated view on the Agile board in Agile Development 2.0.
-   **[Run tests for a story in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/run-test-plan-within-agile.md)**  
For a story whose tests are marked ready to be run, run those tests and execute all the test steps using the Sprint Tracking tab of the Agile board in Agile Development 2.0.
-   **[View the test results for a story in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/view-test-result-form.md)**  
Analyze results of the tests that you run on Agile Development 2.0 stories. Troubleshoot failures so that the story can be moved to completion.
-   **[Report a defect in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/create-defect-report.md)**  
Track issues associated to your product or feature by reporting defects in Agile Development 2.0.
-   **[Create an enhancement request in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/create-enhancement-request.md)**  
Capture enhancement ideas for your feature or product as enhancement requests in Agile Development 2.0 so that you can review and prioritize them later.
-   **[Set up a triage board in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/setup-triage-board.md)**  
Set up your own triage board to view and manage records that are important to your product, such as problems, incident tasks, defects, or change requests at one place easily.
-   **[Triage items and add them to a backlog in Agile Development 2.0](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/it-business-management/agile-development/add-triage-board-items-to-backlog-agile-development-2.md)**  
Review the list of items in your triage board and add them to your Agile Development 2.0 backlog. Based on their priority, you can schedule them in the upcoming sprints for your product teams to work on them.
