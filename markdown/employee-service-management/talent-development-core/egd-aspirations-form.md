# Talent Aspirations reference

Administrators manage aspirations used by managers and employees.

<table id="table_es5_2cj_twb"><thead><tr><th>

Field

</th><th>

Description

</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>

**Aspiration type**

</td><td>

Select for a list of aspirations available in your organization. Administrators create different **Aspiration types**. Here are some examples of types of aspirations.-   **General**
-   **Interest**
-   **Position**
-   **Skill**

</td></tr><tr><td>

**Comments**

</td><td>

Comments can be added by you, or people you share your aspiration with.

</td></tr><tr><td>

**Details**

</td><td>

Details of the aspiration can help to keep you on track with the goals you have for the aspiration.

</td></tr><tr><td>

**Employee**

</td><td>

The employee profile number of the employee who created the aspiration.

</td></tr><tr><td>

**Name**

</td><td>

A descriptive name of your aspiration.

</td></tr><tr><td>

**Shared with**

</td><td>

Select from the list of people in your organization with who you can share your aspiration.

</td></tr><tr><td>

**State**

</td><td>

Use the state to track the stages of your aspiration.-   **Draft**
-   **In Progress**
-   **Completed**
-   **Archived**

</td></tr><tr><td>

**Timeframe**

</td><td>

A general guiding time within which you plan to complete your aspiration.-   **Within the next six months**
-   **Within the next year**
-   **Within the next several years**

</td></tr></tbody>
