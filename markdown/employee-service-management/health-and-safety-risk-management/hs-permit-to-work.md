# Permit-to-work system

The permit-to-work system streamlines the management of requests for work permits for potentially hazardous activities and provides an efficient process for issuing these permits.

The permit-to-work system provides a process for workers to submit a permit-to-work request for hazardous activities, receive formal approval, and be issued a permit. A permit is issued after the safety of the job and the site is validated. Potentially hazardous activities might include:

-   Hot work
-   Confined space entry
-   Electrical work
-   Excavation
-   Lifting operations
-   Chemical handling
-   Working at heights
-   Working underwater

Not managing these tasks properly could pose significant risks. The permit-to-work system enhances operational efficiency and promotes the following outcomes:

-   Safety of workers by identifying hazards and executing risk control measures.
-   Accountability by providing a clear trail of documentation when needed for auditing an incident or for legal conformance.
-   Adequate training for workers on risks, safety measures, and permit requirements.
-   Regular monitoring and supervision on safety conformance and permit conditions throughout the duration of the activity.
