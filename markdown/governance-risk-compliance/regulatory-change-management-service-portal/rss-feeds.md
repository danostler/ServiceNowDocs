# RSS feeds overview

A Really simple syndication \(RSS\) feed in Regulatory Change Management is like a subscription service for updates about new or changed rules and regulations. Instead of visiting multiple websites to check for updates, you can subscribe to feeds from regulatory bodies or industry news.

When a new regulation is published, it automatically appears in your feed, for example, getting notifications from a news app. This ability helps companies stay on top of important changes quickly and easily, ensuring they remain compliant with the latest rules.

## Key benefits of RSS feeds

-   **Automated updates**

    RSS feeds help compliance professionals monitor regulatory changes without manually checking multiple websites or sources.

-   **Centralized information**

    By aggregating feeds from various regulatory bodies, companies can centralize all updates in one place, making it easier to track changes.

-   **Timely alerts**

    Subscribing to relevant feeds ensures that stakeholders receive near real-time notifications of new regulations or amendments, which is critical for staying compliant.

-   **Customization**

    Users can tailor their feeds to focus on specific regions, industries, or types of regulatory changes relevant to their operations.


## RSS feed

A financial institution might subscribe to RSS feeds from regulatory bodies like the SEC, FINRA, or the European Central Bank. Updates from these feeds would inform the compliance team of new rules or guidelines affecting their operations. By leveraging RSS feeds, organizations can reduce the risk of missing critical updates and streamline their regulatory compliance processes.
