# Submit a privacy request using the external facing PDR form

Submit a privacy request using the external-facing Personal Data Rights \(PDR\) form.

## Before you begin

Role required: none.

This form is generally embedded into an organization's website and is publicly available to customers, ex-employees, and third-party Individuals.

## About this task

After submitting your privacy request, verify your email address using a six-digit code. On successful verification, your request is processed and you receive an acknowledgment email with your PDR case number.

## Procedure

1.  Append `/now/grc/portal/public/pdr` to your organization's servicenow instance URL \(for example, `https://<instance-name>.service-now.com/now/grc/portal/public/pdr`\).

2.  In the **Country** field, select your country of residence.

3.  If applicable, select your state or region.

    The form automatically displays the privacy rights available for your jurisdiction.

4.  In the **I am a** field, choose your relationship to the organization \(for example, customer or ex-employee\).

5.  In the **Request Type** field, select the privacy right you wish to exercise.

6.  Provide a detailed **Request Description** to clarify your request, and select **Next**.

7.  In the Requester details page, provide contact information and select **Next**.

8.  Review the information you've provided, and select **Continue**.

    A six-digit verification code is sent to your email address.

9.  Enter the six-digit verification code sent to your email address and select **Submit**.


## Result

On successful OTP validation, your privacy request is submitted for processing.You will receive an acknowledgment email containing your PDR case number and confirmation that your request has been received. The privacy team will review your request and respond within the timeframe required by applicable privacy laws.

**Note:**

-   If the verification code is entered incorrectly or not provided, your request will not be processed. For security reasons, you cannot retry the verification code again on the same submission.
-   You must start a new request if verification fails. This approach helps protect your data and the organization’s systems from unauthorized or repeated attempts.
