# Task page and List view

The Tasks page in Operational Resilience Workspace provides a single-pane view of your pending tasks, your group's tasks, and the tasks that are on your watchlist. You can also update the tasks directly from the dashboard. On the List page, you can configure your operational resilience tasks.

## Tabs in the Tasks page

The Tasks page in Operational Resilience Workspace displays the following tabs:

-   **My pending tasks**
-   **My items**
-   **Watchlist**

## My pending tasks

The **My pending tasks** tab displays an overview of the pending tasks that are assigned to you and the tasks that require an action from you.

**Note:** The data displayed in the Tasks page is based on the roles of the users.

The **My pending tasks** tab displays your pending tasks in different states. You can filter your pending tasks by using the following types of the states:

-   All
-   Draft/New
-   In progress
-   Needs my approval
-   Needs my review
-   Overdue
-   Pending response

## Watchlist

The **Watchlist** tab displays a list of the tasks that are on your watchlist. When a task is approved or completed, you receive a notification on the tab. The following example shows the updates on the assessments tasks that are on the signed-in user's watchlist.

\[Omitted image "watchlist.png"\] Alt text: My watchlist.

**Note:** On the **My pending tasks**, **My items**, and **Watchlist** tabs, you can only view the records that are in-progress. On the **My pending tasks**, **My items**, and **Watchlist** tabs, the closed and canceled records aren’t displayed. In Operational Resilience Workspace, you can view the closed and canceled records on their respective forms.

If you have the Operational Resilience administrator role, you can customize Operational Resilience Workspace by updating the Tasks page configurations. To update the Tasks page configurations, you can navigate to **All &gt; GRC Landing Page Configurations &gt; Tasks Page Configuration** in your instance.

If you have the Operational Resilience manager or Operational Resilience user roles, you can also get relevant recommendations on your resilience tasks on the landing page.

To view more information on the Tasks Page Configuration, see [Update the Tasks Page Configuration record](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/governance-risk-compliance/grc-common-functions/view-tasks-page-configurations.md).

## List view in Operational Resilience Workspace

From the List view, you can set up a business service, analyze its importance and impact tolerance, perform a scenario analysis, and generate a self-attestation report.

For more information on setting up a business service in Operational Resilience Workspace, see [Data setup for business services](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/governance-risk-compliance/setting-up-services-in-ws.md).

For more information on analyzing the importance and impact tolerance of a business service in Operational Resilience Workspace, see [Performing Importance and impact tolerance assessment](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/governance-risk-compliance/analyze-impact-tolerance-importance-of-service-in-ws.md).

For more information on performing a scenario analysis in Operational Resilience Workspace, see [Conducting a scenario analysis](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/governance-risk-compliance/scenario-analysis-in-ws.md).

For more information on self-attesting your business services in Operational Resilience Workspace, see [Certifying services using self-attestation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ServiceNow/ServiceNowDocs/zurich/markdown/zurich/governance-risk-compliance/self-attestation-in-ws.md).
